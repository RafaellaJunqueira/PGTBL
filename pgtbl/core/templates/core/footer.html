Team Based Learning

{% blocktrans %} Team-based learning or TBL is a collaborative learning teaching strategy that focuses on a three-step cycle: Preparation, Class or RAT Assessment, and Small Application-Focused Project {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans 'Preparation' %}

{% blocktrans %}Basically, the teacher will provide articles and books for study, in which the students will be able to download, this material will be the preparation for the evaluation, in addition to having an area for resolution of the list of exercises.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans 'Individual test: iRAT' %}

iRAT {% blocktrans %} Answered individually without consultation or any material, it consists of 10 to 20 questions of multiple choice contemplating the most relevant concepts of the readings or activities indicated previously, each question will have 4 alternatives and the student will be able to distribute points among the alternatives, for example if in doubt between alternative A and C, you can distribute your 4 points in 2 in A and 2 in C, if the correct answer is to C you get 2 points out of 4 and the sum of all points divided by the total points of the test is calculated the student's grade {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans 'Team test: gRAT' %}

gRAT: {% blocktrans %} Answered in a group without consultation or any material, consists of the same questions of the iRAT, each alternative of the question the student will have to specify the order of choice, for example, among the 4 alternatives of the question he places 4 points in the first alternative, if it is correct the student receives a total score that is 4, if the correct one is the alternative with 2 points the student receives 2 points, 1 point equals 1 point and 0 points equals no point in the question, after the student will have immediate feedbacks of the correct answer through scratch card, the sum of all points divided by the total is calculated by the group note in that evaluation. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans 'Appels' %}

{% blocktrans %} Teams may appeal if they do not agree with the correct answer, all appeals must be accompanied by arguments, suggestions for improvement and consultation with relevant bibliographic sources. The team should also propose the new format and the correct answer to the question. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans 'Instructor feedback' %}

{% blocktrans %} After the assessments and the appeals the instructor will pick up the questions that the students missed the most and give feedback about them. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans 'Application oriented activities' %}

{% blocktrans %} The application-centric project executes all the knowledge acquired in a real context of the proposed topic. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans 'Pair test' %}

{% blocktrans %} During the semester peer evaluations may take place in which each member of the team will evaluate the other team members, a maximum score will be provided for each member to distribute among the other members of the team and a field to say why the score may enter feedback for the teammate, this evaluation does not need to identify if it will be anonymous and the score will be part of the individual note of each team member. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans 'Advantages' %}

{% trans 'The use of TBL has some advantages' %}:

  1. {% trans 'Collaboration' %}: {% trans 'The main objective is the learning in teams, one helping the other' %}
  2. {% trans 'Entertainment' %}: {% blocktrans %} The software has an idea of gamefication, having competition between teams so that the teams can train the collaboration between them. {% endblocktrans %}
  3. {% trans 'Training' %}: {% blocktrans %} Professionals more qualified for the job market, because the methodology makes the student to study constantly and work as a team. {% endblocktrans %}
  4. {% trans 'Innovation' %}: {% trans 'Get out of the cold and try something new' %}
  5. {% trans 'Teaching learning' %}: {% blocktrans %} The student becomes more participative and motivated to learn and the teacher makes the class more active and interesting {% endblocktrans %}
